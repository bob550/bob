The knight showed Robin presently his treasury, and again implored him to take the four
hundred pieces of gold, if he would take no interest. But his guest was firm: "Keep the
money, for it is your own. I have but made the Bishop return to you that which he had
first stolen from your hands."
Sir Richard again expressed his thanks, and now led them to his armory. Therein Robin
saw, placed apart, a hundred strong bows with fine waxen silk strings, and a hundred
sheaves of arrows. Every shaft was an ell long, and dressed with peacock's feathers and
notched with silver. Beside them were a hundred suits of red and white livery, finely
made and stitched. "These are the poor presents we have made for you, Robin," said Sir
Richard. "Take them from us, with ten thousand times their weight in gratitude."
One of the knight's own men came forward to give a sheaf of the arrows into Robin's
hand, and, behold, it was Arthur- à-Bland!
