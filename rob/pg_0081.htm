The outlaw held up his right hand and laid it on Robin's bowed head: "Upon you, Robin of

"Farewell—f riends all—take this brother into your good company, and make him and

His speech fell away into meaningless words, and the light left his face. He moved in
Robin's arms and sighed. Then, as his body rolled slowly over, and he lay with his back
turned to them, they saw that his worst wound was in it—a dastard's blow. So ended the
life of Will o' th' Green—or Will of Cloudesley: he of whom many stories have been told
in other books.
They took him up reverently and bur ied him in a secret place—so that none to this day
